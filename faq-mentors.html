FAQs for Summer Fellowship Mentor Applicants

Apply to be a Summer Mentor

How do I apply?

Fill out the application form by February 1, 2014

What kind of mentors are you looking for?

We're looking for data scientists with strong technical skills and practical experience with an interest in mentoring students learn real-world skills and make a social impact. Last year, we had a mix of people from Industry and Academia (with practical, real-world experience) as mentors with Computer Science, Machine Learning, Statistics, Economics, Applied Math and Physics backgrounds

Is it a full-time program and do I have to be in Chicago?

Yes, we are looking for full-time mentors who can work with our team of fellows for the summer in Chicago. While we are grateful for part-time, volunteer help if you really want to help us, we are in need to dedicated, full-time, experienced mentors who can spend the entire summer in Chicago with our team of fellows.

When does the fellowship start and finish?

We'll start end of May and finish end of August. We're working on figuring out multiple start dates that can accomodate different end of quarter/semester dates

What if i can only help part-time?

You should still email us and/or apply. We'll keep you informed on how we can use your help but we really need good, full-time mentors.

Do I get paid?

Yes, we do have funding for mentors. Feel free to email us with more questions about that.

When do I hear back from the fellowship?

We'll review applications starting February 1 and will schedule interviews after we go through a round of reviewing. Final decisions will be sent out by March 1.

What projects am I going to be working on?

Call for Projects is still on-going. You can see projects from last year here. Projects will be in partnership with government and non profit organizations and will be in areas of education, healthcare, energy, community development, disaster relief, and public safety. We'll send out a specific list of projects with our decision by March 15.
